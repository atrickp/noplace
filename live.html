An afternoon of celebrating the infinite possibilities of the human voice with the WORLD PREMIERE of VOICE BAND

Voice band is:
Matthew Rahaim
Anat Spiegel
Lizz Windnagel
Adam Zahller

I have seen each of these four artists use their voices in incredibly beautiful and novel ways, often in improvised settings, usually with other people playing instruments (aka performers using physical objects and NOT using their lungs, mouths, noses, and/or vocal cords to produce sound). Seeing all of them as the sole “Vocalists” in these scenarios got me thinking: what would happen if they could improvise *exclusively* with other vocalists? I became kind of obsessed with the idea, and to my delight, all of them were interested in developing the concept. During our conversations I came to refer to the idea as ~*Voice Band*~, and I guess it kind of stuck...

Originally I wanted to call this show “Voice Is The Original Instrument” — partially in reference to Joan La Barbara's 1976 album of the same name (you should listen to it, it is incredible!) but mostly because I felt like it summed up why I was excited about having these incredible musicians make sound together. But the more I sat with it the more I felt like it didn't quite get at what I was feeling at the time.

“Voice Is The Original Instrument” is a very emphatic and provocative declaration, probably intentionally so considering the context La Barbara was creating work at the time*. But independent of that context and interpreted on its own it does lead to many questions: Is voice the original instrument? What makes something an “instrument?” What does “original” mean? Whose voice?...

And after thinking about that for a while I came to a realization that brought me closer to what most excites me about Voice Band: of human's many tools for self-expression, voice is one of few that is embedded within our bodies. It is something we don't have to build or buy. It is something most of us use every day whether or not we consider it an apparatus for beauty making. And just like other parts of our bodies, it is unique to each of us. And my impulse to ask these four amazing artists to make some sounds with each other is to truly celebrate the infinite possibilities (literally) within all of us!

So I guess I could have called the group “Voice is the timbrealy unique mechanism, miraculously evolved in humans over millennia, that seems to directly channel emotions from one individual to another as if through the ether, and can do so across the boundaries of time, space, and language, intuitively but with a vast potential for technical development over the course of a lifetime...”

…but I think we'll stick with Voice Band :)

Join us for the *World Premiere* performance of Voice Band in my backyard on July 24. 2pm Doors (and maybe some snacks?) 3pm Performance. Post-performance hang and open grill (bring something to cook!) I think it is going to be very fun and cool! RSVP or DM for address!

--Patrick Marschke (house show host and #1 Voice Band fan and advocate)
